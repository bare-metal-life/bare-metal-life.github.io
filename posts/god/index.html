God and/versus Religion

There is only one definition of the word “God” that makes sense to me:

That which caused the existence of the cosmos, including me.

  • I exist
  • The Cosmos exists
  • Something caused the Cosmos and me to exist
  • That Something gets the name “God”

This concept of God radically simplifies this thing we call “Religion”.

It is simply about connecting with the Creator.

The Cosmos could be self-creating but to my mind this is absurd. There is simply too much information embedded in everything that exists.

The Cosmos has a rational structure that can be modelled using mathematics. Patterns can be observed and predicted. The Cause of the Cosmos imbued it with a rational nature that can be discerned by human beings.

Beyond that, I do not believe we need to theorise about God. We do not need huge books filled with theological reasoning. We do not need to ‘study’ God.

If God is the Creator of all that is, then surely an uneducated peasant in a muddy field would have just as much ability to connect with his Creator as some scholar with a dozen academic credentials?

This is my understanding of the “Good News” of Jesus Christ: that the Creator relates to us like a good father to his children, not as a stony-faced judge or an impersonal force that created the Cosmos and then just left it to run on by itself.

This is why I eventually found my way to the Orthodox Church … because of the simplicity of its teaching and its refusal to indulge in pointless intellectual theorising about the nature of God.
