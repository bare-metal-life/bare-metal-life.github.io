Anna Karenina

The book on my mother’s bedside table I first encountered Anna when I was 17. I saw the book on my mother’s bedside table. The title immediately attracted me. Anna Karenina. The name sounded beautiful, Russian and mysterious. I started reading: Happy families are all alike; every unhappy family is unhappy in its own way. If I’m not hooked by the first line of a book, it’s usually a sign I will struggle with the rest of the book.
