More Thoughts on Language

More thoughts on language – In which I describe some observations about how people use foreign languages, and explain my motivation for learning Thai and Russian. This post was inspired by a video produced by Maria, my Russian phonetics coach. She described three types of Russian language learner: The linguistic sportsman who conquers languages in the way Genghis Khan conquered territory The person who in everyday life is Roger but in his heart is Dostoevsky – who is in love with the “Russian Soul” The ‘spy’, who lives among Russians and who wants to sound like a native when he speaks.

Albania

I have always had a soft spot for Albania. They always seemed to end up being the underdogs of the Balkans, and also I have only ever had positive interactions with Albanians. Albanians have the misfortune to live on the Balkan peninsula at the same time as not being Slavs, Greeks or Turks. They have had to somehow carve out living space for themselves in competition with the Serbs in particular – the situation in Kosovo being the eventual result.

Mystery

My teenage years were dominated by the intensity of the Cold War. At the other end of Europe, there was this strange, unfamiliar place behind the “Iron Curtain”. With my shortwave receiver I had static-filled access to signals from “Behind the Iron Curtain”. It was a place of sinister mystery from which I could listen to broadcasts denouncing Imperialists and Fascists as the sun set and atmospheric conditions allowed the signals to penetrate more strongly.
